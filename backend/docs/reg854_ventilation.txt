O. Reg 854 s.118 - Ventilation and airflow underground

NOTE: Excerpted plain-language working copy for offline edge use. Not the
official consolidated regulation.

Ventilation is the primary control for gas underground: you dilute and remove
hazardous gases by moving enough fresh air through each working zone. When
airflow drops, gases that were being safely carried away begin to accumulate,
which is why a falling airflow reading frequently precedes a rising gas reading.

(1) A mechanical ventilation system shall be provided and operated to supply
each underground workplace with air of sufficient quantity and quality to
dilute and render harmless any airborne hazardous substances and to provide a
working environment that does not endanger a worker's health.

(2) The quantity of air shall be sufficient for the number of workers and the
diesel equipment in use in the zone; where diesel engines operate, additional
airflow is required to dilute exhaust gases.

(3) Where the ventilation system fails or airflow falls below the planned
quantity for a zone, work that generates gas or that depends on dilution shall
stop until adequate airflow is restored.

Trend guidance: treat declining airflow together with rising methane or CO as a
single developing hazard, not two separate readings.
