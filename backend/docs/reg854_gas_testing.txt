O. Reg 854 s.119 - Testing the atmosphere for gas

NOTE: Excerpted plain-language working copy for offline edge use. Not the
official consolidated regulation.

You cannot manage what you do not measure. Continuous and spot testing of the
mine atmosphere is what makes trend detection possible in the first place.

(1) The atmosphere of an underground workplace shall be tested for oxygen
content and for hazardous gases as often as necessary to ensure the health and
safety of workers, and in any case before workers enter a place that may
contain a hazardous atmosphere.

(2) Testing shall be carried out with instruments that are appropriate for the
gases that may be present, that are maintained in good working order, and that
are calibrated at the intervals recommended by the manufacturer.

(3) The results of atmospheric testing shall be recorded, and where a result
indicates a developing hazard the supervisor shall be notified so that
corrective action can be taken.

Trend guidance: a single reading is a snapshot; a sequence of readings is the
signal. Retaining recent readings per zone is what lets the system distinguish
a stable atmosphere from one that is rising toward a limit.
