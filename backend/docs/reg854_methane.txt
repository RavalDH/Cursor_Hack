O. Reg 854 s.123 - Methane (flammable gas) limits and worker withdrawal

NOTE: This file is an excerpted, plain-language working copy of the relevant
parts of Ontario Regulation 854 (Mines and Mining Plants) for offline use at
the edge. It is for demo/decision-support and is not a substitute for the
official consolidated regulation.

Where methane or another flammable gas is present in the atmosphere of an
underground workplace, the following applies:

(1) Where the concentration of methane reaches 1.0 per cent by volume in the
general body of air, the supervisor shall be notified and the cause shall be
investigated and corrected. Ventilation shall be checked and increased as
required to dilute and carry away the gas.

(2) Where the concentration of methane reaches 1.25 per cent by volume,
electrical equipment that is not flameproof in the affected area shall be
de-energized, and no further drilling, blasting or other ignition-capable work
shall be carried on.

(3) Where the concentration of methane reaches 2.5 per cent by volume, all
workers shall be withdrawn from the affected area to a safe place in fresh air,
the area shall be barricaded against entry, and work shall not resume until the
concentration has been reduced and the area declared safe by a supervisor.

Trend guidance: a methane concentration that is rising toward these limits is
itself a warning condition. Workers should not wait for a fixed alarm value
before acting where the gas is clearly climbing in a poorly ventilated area.
