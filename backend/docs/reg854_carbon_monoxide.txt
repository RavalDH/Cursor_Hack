O. Reg 854 s.124 - Carbon monoxide (CO) exposure and response

NOTE: Excerpted plain-language working copy for offline edge use. Not the
official consolidated regulation.

Carbon monoxide is a colourless, odourless gas produced by combustion - diesel
equipment, blasting fumes, and underground fires. It is dangerous because it
binds to blood far more readily than oxygen, so workers can be incapacitated
before they sense anything is wrong.

(1) The time-weighted average exposure to carbon monoxide shall not exceed the
occupational exposure limit of 25 parts per million over an eight-hour shift.

(2) Where carbon monoxide reaches 35 parts per million, the source shall be
identified and ventilation increased; the supervisor shall be notified.

(3) Where carbon monoxide reaches 100 parts per million, workers in the
affected area shall don escape respirators and withdraw to fresh air, and the
area shall be ventilated and re-tested before re-entry.

Because CO often signals a fire or running engine in a confined space, a rising
CO trend shall be treated as a possible fire indication and reported
immediately.
