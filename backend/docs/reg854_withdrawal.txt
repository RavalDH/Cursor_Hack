O. Reg 854 s.26 - Withdrawal of workers from a dangerous place

NOTE: Excerpted plain-language working copy for offline edge use. Not the
official consolidated regulation.

This section is the backbone of the early-warning response: when a place
underground becomes dangerous, the duty is to get people out first and fix the
condition second.

(1) Where the health or safety of a worker is or is likely to be endangered,
the worker shall be withdrawn from the dangerous place to a safe location, and
the dangerous place shall be barricaded or otherwise guarded against
inadvertent entry.

(2) No worker shall be permitted to enter or remain in the dangerous place
except for the purpose of correcting the condition, and then only with adequate
protective equipment and under the direction of a supervisor.

(3) Work shall not resume in the affected place until the condition that
created the danger has been corrected and the place has been examined and
declared safe.

Early-warning intent: acting on a rising trend allows an orderly withdrawal
well before the atmosphere reaches a level that would force an emergency
evacuation.
