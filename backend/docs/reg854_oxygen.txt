O. Reg 854 s.120 - Oxygen content of the underground atmosphere

NOTE: Excerpted plain-language working copy for offline edge use. Not the
official consolidated regulation.

Workers underground depend on the ventilation system to maintain a breathable
atmosphere. Oxygen can be displaced by other gases (for example after a blast or
from an inrush) or consumed by oxidation, fire, or diesel engines. Unlike the
flammable and toxic gases, with oxygen it is a LOW reading that signals danger.

(1) The oxygen content of the air in an underground workplace shall be maintained
at a normal level by ventilation. Normal fresh air is about 20.9 per cent oxygen
by volume.

(2) Where the oxygen concentration falls below approximately 19.5 per cent, the
condition shall be treated as an oxygen-deficiency warning: ventilation shall be
increased, the cause of the deficiency investigated, and the supervisor notified.

(3) Where the oxygen concentration falls to a dangerous level (approximately 18
per cent or below), workers shall not enter or remain in the area without
supplied-air breathing apparatus. The area shall be ventilated and re-tested
before normal re-entry.

Trend guidance: a falling oxygen reading alongside rising carbon monoxide or
methane is a single developing hazard — often a fire, a blast, or a gas inrush —
and shall be acted on together, not as separate readings.
